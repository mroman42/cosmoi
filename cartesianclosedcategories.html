Cartesian closed categories

A cartesian closed category can be defined as a category having a right adjoint of the unique functor to the terminal category , a right adjoint of the diagonal functor , and a right adjoint of for each . These three adjoints correspond to the existence of a terminal object, binary products and exponentials.

These three rules match the three introduction rules for the simply typed lambda calculus. We interpret as a context and each morphism as a term .
