Cartesian closed categories

A cartesian closed category \(\mathbb{C}\) can be defined as a category having a right adjoint of the unique functor to the terminal category \(\ast \colon \mathbb{C} \to 1\), a right adjoint of the diagonal functor \(\Delta \colon \mathbb{C} \to \mathbb{C} \times \mathbb{C}\), and a right adjoint of \(- \times A \colon \mathbb{C} \to \mathbb{C}\) for each \(A \in \mathbb{C}\). These three adjoints correspond to the existence of a terminal object, binary products and exponentials.

These three rules match the three introduction rules for the simply typed lambda calculus. We interpret \(C\) as a context \(\Gamma\) and each morphism \(a \colon C \to A\) as a term \(\Gamma \vdash a : A\).
